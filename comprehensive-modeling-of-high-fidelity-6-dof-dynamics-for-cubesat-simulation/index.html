Comprehensive Modeling of High-Fidelity 6-DOF Dynamics for CubeSat Simulation

CubeSats, standardized small satellites with a 10 cm cubic form factor (1U, scaling to 12U), have transformed space exploration by enabling cost-effective missions for academia, industry, and government. Their compact size (1.33–16 kg) supports applications from Earth observation to interplanetary missions. A high-fidelity 6-DOF (six degrees of freedom) model, capturing three translational and three rotational degrees of freedom, is essential for accurate simulation. This article provides an exhaustive exploration of CubeSat dynamics, including equations for translational and rotational motion, environmental perturbations, sensor and actuator models, control strategies, and verification and validation (V&V) considerations. It includes detailed examples and prepares for a future Python-based simulation using NumPy, SciPy, and Matplotlib, tailored for aerospace engineers, GNC specialists, and CubeSat developers.

Introduction to 6-DOF Dynamics

A CubeSat’s motion comprises translational dynamics (position and velocity in 3D space) and rotational dynamics (attitude and angular velocity). The 6-DOF model integrates these, accounting for forces like gravity, atmospheric drag, and solar radiation pressure, and torques such as gravity gradient, magnetic, and aerodynamic. High-fidelity modeling includes environmental perturbations, sensor noise, actuator dynamics, and control algorithms, critical for attitude and orbit control system (AOCS) design. This article elaborates on the mathematical framework, environmental models, sensor and actuator dynamics, control strategies, and V&V, with examples, aligning with GNC practices from projects like MIURA 5 and ISRO’s satellite missions.
